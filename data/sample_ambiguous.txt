Subject: GRC Platform and Controls Program — Scope Uncertain

Sterling Mutual Insurance, a US-based mid-market insurance carrier (~$900M in premiums), is seeking a partner for a combined initiative around its governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) function. The request is broad and not fully scoped. On one hand, they want to implement and configure a new GRC technology platform (likely Workiva or AuditBoard) to replace spreadsheets — system selection, configuration, integration, and rollout. On the other hand, they also describe wanting help maturing their control framework, performing a readiness assessment ahead of a future SOC 2 examination, and advising on risk governance structure (committees, charters, accountability). They have not decided whether this is primarily a technology implementation or an advisory/controls engagement, and have asked the firm to help them shape the scope. Executive sponsor is the Chief Risk Officer. They want to begin within the quarter.
